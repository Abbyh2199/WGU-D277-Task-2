About Florida

  • Population: 22.61 million (2023)
  • Capital: Tallahassee
  • State Bird: Mockingbird
  • Counties: 67
  • State Flower: Orange Blossom
  • Nickname: The Sunshine State

Florida is a state located in the southeastern region of the United States. The state borders the Gulf of Mexico to the west, Alabama to the northwest, Georgia to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, and the Straits of Florida and Cuba to the south. The state's capital is Tallahasseee and its most populous municipality is Jacksonville.

Florida was first inhabited by indigenous peoples for thousands of years before it was discovered by European explorers. Florida was a Spanish colony for more than 200 years before it became a territory of the United States in 1821. Florida became the 27th state of the United States in 1845. During the American Civil War, Florida seceded from the Union and joined the Confederate States of America. Florida was readmitted to the Union in 1868.
