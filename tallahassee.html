About Tallahassee

  • Population: 202,221 (2023)
  • Year Incorporated: 1824
  • Region: Northern Panhandle
  • Classification: Urban
  • Average Income $55,931 (2023)

Tallahassee, city, capital of Florida, U.S., and seat (1824) of Leon county. It is situated in the central part of the state’s northern panhandle region about halfway between Pensacola (west) and Jacksonville (east).

In 1821, when Florida became an American territory, it had two capitals, St. Augustine and Pensacola. As a central location between the two, Tallahassee (derived from a Creek word meaning “old town”) became the capital in 1824.
